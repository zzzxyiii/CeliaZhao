Research and design practice of residual neural network muscle fatigue prediction model for overhead work

Background

Work-related musculosketetal disorders(WMSDs), it refers to health problems of the musculoskeletal and nervous systems among professional people caused or aggravated by poor postures, long-term force application and repetitive operations and other poor ergonomic factors during work. It encompasses all forms of health-disease conditions ranging from mild and temporary injuries to irreversible and disabling injuries.

Overhead work, It is usually defined as performing hand tasks when the hand is above the shoulder or when the flexion and extension angle of the shoulder joint is greater than 60 degrees. Working in this posture not only reduces work capacity and efficiency but also lowers the precision of task completion.

  • Artificial Neural Network(ANNs) models can handle complex relationships, especially nonlinear regression relationships, learn patterns from multiple variables, and make predictions based on existing data. The introduction of neural network models enables the model to more precisely capture the complex interactions among multiple variables, thereby more accurately predicting the relationship between muscle fatigue and work efficiency.
Research Process

Based on the data obtained from experimental analysis, we established a neural network model to predict muscle fatigue, thereby reducing the risk of WMSDs. The research consists of four key phases.

1
Mechanical Experiment Analysis

Recruited 16 male university students as participants for the experiment, and simulated the overhead work environment and measured data from three dimensions--MVC, MET, maximum residual muscle strength.

2
ResNet Building

Analyzed 15 leading smart home products and platforms to identify strengths and weaknesses.

3
model validation

Organized 5 co-design sessions with users and stakeholders to generate and refine ideas.

4
Wrist Brace Design

Iteratively tested 4 prototype versions with 20 users to validate design decisions.

Validation & Results

The ResNet model exhibited outstanding predictive accuracy, with a root mean square error(RMSE) of 0.028. Compared with traditional backpropagration neural networks(RMSE=0.053) and multilayer perceptron neural networks(RMSE=0.059), they displayed smaler errors and better fitting.

The propsed residual neural network muscle fatigue prediction model can effectively and accurately predict muscle fatigue, providing strong support for improving work efficiency and reducing the risk of work-related musculoskeletalo disorders.

Design Model & Product

The model was applied to an example to verify the feasibility of the prediction model, and the optimization design and improvement of wrist joint protector was proposed. Through the redesign and optimization of wrist brace, the degree of muscle fatigue of wrist joint could be effectively reduced, thus reducing the incidence of WMSDs.

We evaluated these sketches using the PUGH matrix, collecting scores from a decision-making team consisting of 10 design graduate students and 5 design experts.

Key Features:
  • An X-shaped support backplate
  • A ResNet muscle fatigue prediction model processor
  • A red light indicates a muscle fatigue warning
  • A yellow indicator light displays three levels of fatigue alerts
  • 3D high-elastic breathable fabric

Research on the Design of Upper-Limb-Assisting Exoskeletons for Overhead Work

Background

In recent years, the technology of exoskeletons for human bodies has been continuously improving, and the types and quantities of exoskeletons have been increasing. However, there are relatively few exoskeletons suitable for maintenance work involving hand-overhead operations. Maintenance personnel often perform repetitive hand-overhead operations for a long time, and they need a suitable auxiliary tool to assist them in maintenance operations and alleviate muscle and bone strain. There is still a certain gap in this field.

From the perspective of power source, upper limb assistive exoskeletons can be classified into two types: active upper limb assistive exoskeletons and passive upper limb assistive exoskeletons. Passive upper limb assistive exoskeletons do not require external energy. They usually generate assistive functions by releasing the stored energy through elastic energy storage components. The source of the assistance is generally the elastic potential energy of springs, coil springs, and elastic soft materials, etc.

Research Process

This project was divided into four phases.

1
Ergonomics Analysis

We conducted ergonomic analysis through literature research, which mainly included upper body size, anatomy, joint range of motion analyses, behavior analysis during overhead work.

2
Design of the Exoskeleton System

The exoskeleton scheme was designed with the purpose of providing assistance in the overhand range and no assistance in other ranges, which was specifically achieved through the cooperation of cams, connecting rods and torsion springs, change the change curve of the support force to provide better assistance for overhand operations.

3
Dynamics Simulation Analysis

We input the rotation angle range of each joint of the exoskeleton into the program to simulate its workspace range and Adams dynamic simulation analysis.

4
User Experience Evaluation

we used EMG (electromyographic) signals; for the subjective evaluation, we adopted the Borg Scale.

Design Model & Product

There is the CMF design and final renderings of the exoskeleton.

The system includes the overall scheme of the exoskeleton and the design details of its main parts.

System Components:
  • The structure and operation mode of the cam and connecting rod sliding state
  • Followed by the degree of freedom scheme design
  • The linkage between the cam and the shaft
  • Combining the "human-machine-environment" system
  • practical application factors
Validation & Results

The ResNet model exhibited outstanding predictive accuracy, with a root mean square error(RMSE) of 0.028. Compared with traditional backpropagration neural networks(RMSE=0.053) and multilayer perceptron neural networks(RMSE=0.059), they displayed smaler errors and better fitting.

Then, we processed the EMG signals with a second-order IIR Butterworth filter (30-400Hz high-channel filtering) to reduce the impact of heart rate and other muscle signals.
